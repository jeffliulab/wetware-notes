# Wetware Computing

Wetware computing treats living neuronal networks as a computing substrate — cultured neurons that learn, adapt, and compute. It is the "computing with living tissue" layer that unifies neuroscience, cognitive science, and the biological substrate into a new brain-like computing paradigm.

## Chapter Outline

1. **Introduction** — Definition, motivation, and landscape of wetware computing.
2. **In-Vitro Neuronal Networks** — Growing computable neuronal networks in a dish.
3. **Stimulation & Multi-Electrode Recording** — Interface technology to read and write living neural networks.
4. **In-Vitro Learning & Plasticity** — How cultured neurons learn — the DishBrain paradigm.
5. **Biohybrid & Closed-Loop Systems** — Embedding living networks in perception-action loops.
6. **Physical & Reservoir Computing** — Paradigms that treat biological dynamics as a computing resource.
7. **Ethics & Frontiers** — Ethical boundaries and future directions of wetware intelligence.
